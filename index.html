Visualizing Your 180-Mark Goal

To score 180 out of 300, you need to correctly answer 45 questions (45 * 4 = 180), assuming no negative marking. This donut chart shows your 60% target, which translates to a strong focus on high-weightage chapters.

Physics: Chapter Weightage

Physics often features a balanced paper, but modern physics, electricity, and mechanics are consistent high-scorers. This chart shows the average number of questions asked per chapter based on past trends.

Chemistry: Chapter Weightage

Chemistry is often the most scoring subject. Focus on Physical (Electrochemistry, Thermodynamics) and Organic (GOC, Name Reactions) as they carry significant weight, along with key Inorganic chapters.

Mathematics: Chapter Weightage

Mathematics can be lengthly. Prioritizing chapters like 3D & Vectors, Calculus, and Conic Sections is crucial. These topics form the bulk of the paper and are essential for reaching your target score.

Strategic Focus for 180+ Marks

A 180-mark target is achievable with a smart strategy. It's not about studying everything, but studying the right things well.

Phase 1: High-Priority Chapters

Master the top 50-60% of the syllabus shown in the charts. Aim for 100% accuracy in these "must-do" chapters from all three subjects.

Phase 2: Mock Tests & Analysis

Start taking full-length mock tests early. Your goal is to consistently score 180-200. Analyze mistakes to identify and fix weak areas.

Phase 3: Medium & Easy Chapters

Secure easy marks from "low-weightage" but simple chapters (e.g., Mathematical Reasoning, EM Waves). These are your rank boosters.
